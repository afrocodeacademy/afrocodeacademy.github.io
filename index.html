Teaching Code to Africans by Africans

Our vision for "Afro Code Academy" is to empower children of African origin with the skills and knowledge to succeed in the digital world. We believe that by teaching these children how to code, we can help them unlock new opportunities and pave the way for a brighter future.

About our Project

Our project, "Afro Code Academy" is run entirely by dedicated volunteers who are passionate about empowering children of African origin with the skills and knowledge to succeed in the digital world. We believe that by teaching these children how to code, we can help them unlock new opportunities and pave the way for a brighter future.

Free to Learn

We are committed to making our coding classes and workshops accessible to all children of African origin, regardless of their financial circumstances. Our courses are completely free to attend, and we have no plans to change this in the foreseeable future. We believe that every child should have the opportunity to learn valuable skills and build a brighter future, and we are dedicated to removing financial barriers that might prevent them from accessing our program.

Why Join Afro Code Academy?

In addition to being free, our courses are also designed to be flexible and accommodate learners of all levels. Whether a child is just starting to learn about coding or is already an experienced programmer, we have classes and workshops that will suit their needs and help them achieve their goals.

What courses do we offer?

We have courses for 3 different age groups and these groups are sub-divided into smaller groups.

  • Ages 4 - 12 : We aim to introduce programming concepts and algorithmic thinking to kids. This will be done through programming of simple games and learning to perform basic computations through fun and games.

  • Ages 13 - 25 : These we expect to have some form of high school and university education but do not necessarily need to have a programming background. We introduce programming concepts and teach basics for data analysis and also software development.

  • Ages > 25 : These could be the working mum or dad, or people without any extensive university education. We aim to introduce them to frontend web design and basic programming concepts. The goal will be to get them to develop a website and possibly get them Freelance jobs if they are adequately motivated.
